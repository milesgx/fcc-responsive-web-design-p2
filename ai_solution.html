Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Ernest Borlaug (1914-2009) was an American agronomist, humanitarian, and Nobel laureate who has been called "the father of the Green Revolution".

Borlaug is credited with saving over a billion people from starvation through his work in developing high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties.

In 1970, Borlaug was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to world peace through increasing food supply.
